Research Interests

I am currently doing research in the Dartmouth Computer Science department with Professor Bailey-Kellogg. I am using computational techniques (clustering algorithms, motif extraction, and different analysis methods) in Python to analyze T-cell receptor sequence data and reveal patterns to help answer questions about similarities and differences in T-cell receptor repertoires in different patient populations. I have been developing a method of processing TCR sequence data in a meaningful way that will enable us to discover interesting patterns.

I have also undertaken previous research projects in immunology at the Hogquist immunology lab at the University of Minnesota, and at the Huang immunology lab at the Geisel School of Medicine. At the Hogquist lab, I used flow cytometry to investigate a correlation between NKG2A+ natural killer cell numbers and Epstein-Barr virus severity of infection in humans. At the Huang lab, I worked on developing a procedure to induce B cells to switch isotype to IgG3, a critical component of a larger project to develop an HIV vaccine.

I love the opportunities that research has given me to explore and better understand our world, and hope to continue contributing to our breadth of scientific knowledge.
