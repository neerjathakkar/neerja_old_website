About Me

Hello! My name is Neerja and I'm a senior at Dartmouth majoring in Computer Science and Mathematics.

I grew up in Minnesota and went to St. Paul Academy and Summit School, during which I loved participating in math competitions and taking science classes. Eager to gain hands-on experience, I did immunology research at the University of Minnesota.

At Dartmouth, I took Intro to Programming and Computation and discovered computer science. I see CS as the perfect tool to explore the world and bridge my interests in math, science and technology. I am fascinated at the way technology affects our lives, and want to use it to better the world we live in. I quickly became interested in research, and fused my interests in CS and immunology by working in the Bailey-Kellogg Lab at Dartmouth.

During my Dartmouth years, I have had several incredible CS-related opportunities: I interned at Facebook and 3M Health Information Systems. I worked as a teaching assistant for three different CS courses. I studied abroad at the Aquincum Institute of Technology in Budapest. I have taken 18 different courses in CS and Math.

During my CS journey at Dartmouth, I discovered computer graphics, and I was hooked. I am deepening my experience in computer graphics and vision research by undertaking a senior thesis project in the Visual Computing Lab. I hope to have a research career, most likely as a professor.

In my free time, I like to play the violin through the Dartmouth Symphony Orchestra and solo studies, dance, enjoy the outdoors, and read.
